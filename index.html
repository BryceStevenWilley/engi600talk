Finding Optimal Robot Motion


Bryce Willey

Automated robots can


  • assist people who might not be mobile



  • collaborate with people

  • do unsafe tasks

Feasible Motion Planning

Find a connected path from start to goal in the configuration space

that doesn't collide with any obstacles

Optimal Motion Planning

Assign a cost to every path: just like money, we want the cost to be low

Find a feasible path that minimized the cost

Pros

If a solution exists, it will find it (but it can't conclusively say "there is no solution.")

Conceptually simple, generalizable

Cons

Must post-process the paths

Optimial Versions exist, but they converge very slowly

Pros

Fast! (5-10x by our experiments)

Quality paths

Cons

Only locally optimal paths

Robot specific

No guarentees for finding a solution

No comparisons in the literature yet

Collision checking takes the majority of the time in motion planning

Optimizing Planners do special 'tricks' to speed up collision checking

Are their speed ups due to these tricks?

Surprisingly, there haven't been any good comparisons in the literature.
